Describe the program or service, including the student needs this proposal would address as well as the goals of this program (Limit: 300 words)

The Open Computing Facility (OCF) is an ASUC-chartered student organization that provides free computing services to students, faculty, and staff, including printing, expiration-free web hosting, Unix shell accounts, and email hosting. The OCF operates a highly frequented long-hours computer lab at 171 MLK with a generally high volunteer-staff-to-user ratio for accessible in-person support, as well as online support. In addition to these services for individual users, the group also hosts numerous student group and faculty/staff websites and provides technical support for them.

The mission of the OCF is to provide an environment where no member of Berkeley's campus community is denied the computer resources they seek, to appeal to all members of the Berkeley campus community with unsatisfied computing needs, and to provide a place for those interested in computing to fully explore that interest. By offering free computing services, we help create equitable access to technology. 

The OCF would not be able to operate without our 7 front desk staff who monitor the lab during open hours. The goal of this proposal is to keep the OCF open and staffed from 9am-8pm, Monday-Friday and 10am-6pm Saturday. Historically, the OCF was wholly supported by the Student Technology Fund (STF), which gave us bandwidth to offer our wide variety of services with a recurring funding source. Since STF’s dissolution we have been exploring various ways to continue our offerings, and while we do utilize ABSA funding for lab supplies, we have not yet found a proper replacement for our front desk staff stipends which are critical to keeping our lab open. So, we are applying for CACSSF funding once again.

Describe the direct benefit(s) to students, which student populations will be served (e.g., graduate students, underserved communities), the estimated number of student who will be impacted, how you arrived at that estimate, and what metrics you will use to measure impact (Limit: 400 words)*

The funding would directly benefit UC Berkeley undergraduate students, graduate students, and faculty, as well as their organizations, by keeping the Open Computing Facility staffed during our regular weekly hours and allowing the facility to maintain its in-person services. Our Operations Staff are stationed at the front desk during their shifts. While there, they check CalIDs, perform printer maintenance, and provide technical assistance to OCF visitors. They also work on weekly projects, which range from designing informational posters to put around the computer lab to cleaning the supply table.

By providing both on-site and off-site computational support for all UC Berkeley faculty and students, the OCF facilitates collaborative computational efforts of all members of the UC Berkeley community, including across departments. And by offering equitable access to printing and computing technology, OCF supports the underserved members of the UC Berkeley community who might otherwise struggle to access these resources which are necessary for academic success. The OCF has been striving to attain its mission for 34 years, and we hope to do so for many more.

As of February 2024, we have 85,080 registered OCF accounts. 2,012 of those are for registered student organizations, labs, and other on-campus groups; the rest are personal accounts of students and faculty. On any given weekday, we have an average of 300-400 unique in-person logins at the lab and 2,888 free pages printed. A total of 85,080 pages have been printed this semester so far. There are 801 active student organizations currently using our web hosting services, over 382 groups using our email hosting service, and 173 students and staff utilizing our high performance computing (HPC) service. We arrived at these estimates based on statistics we track for our printers, as well as account and hosting records. Many of these groups first look to our Operations Staff at the front desk for assistance with anything related to our services, and are positively impacted by the help they provide.

We will continue to use live statistics which are publicly available at https://ocf.io/stats to measure our impact, as well as feedback from our Operational Staff to understand student needs (for example, manilla folders and index cards were highly requested resources last semester that we now provide).

Please provide a timeline for the actions you plan to take if awarded funding, including contingency plans. (Limit: 250 words)

If awarded, the funding would go towards 2024-2025 academic year stipends for our operational staff. Stipends are paid every other week, based on number of hours worked.

Contingency plan: Since we are not allowed to use other sources of funding we receive to pay our student workers, we would have to (1) ask students to volunteer to staff the OCF without pay, (2) significantly reduce the open lab hours of the OCF, and/or (3) apply for emergency funding if available.
